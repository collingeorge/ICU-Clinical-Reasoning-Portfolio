# CTICU Terminology & Abbreviations  
**Comprehensive Attending-Level Reference for Rounds & Case Documentation**  
Version 1.0 — December 2025

---

This document provides the standardized terminology used in the CTICU for hemodynamics, ventilator management, echo, neurocritical care, sedation, shock classification, ECMO, postoperative cardiac care, and critical-care physiology.  
It supports consistent, high-level communication during rounds and in clinical documentation.

---

# 1. Hemodynamic Terminology

### Core Concepts  
- **Preload:** Ventricular filling (volume/pressure) at end-diastole.  
- **Afterload:** Resistance against which the ventricle ejects blood; includes SVR and arterial elastance.  
- **Contractility:** Intrinsic myocardial pumping strength independent of preload/afterload.  
- **Stroke Volume (SV):** Amount of blood ejected per beat.  
- **Cardiac Output (CO):** HR × SV.  
- **Cardiac Index (CI):** CO adjusted for BSA.  
- **MAP (Mean Arterial Pressure):** Organ perfusion pressure; goal ≥ 65 unless neuro-specific.

### Perfusion Metrics  
- **DO₂:** Oxygen delivery.  
- **VO₂:** Oxygen consumption.  
- **ScvO₂ / SvO₂:** Central/mixed venous oxygen saturation (marker of DO₂ adequacy).  
- **Lactate:** Marker of anaerobic metabolism or impaired clearance.  
- **Shock Index:** HR/MAP (≥ 0.7 concerning; ≥ 1.0 suggests shock).  

### Filling Pressures  
- **CVP / RAP:** Central/right atrial pressure.  
- **PCWP:** Pulmonary capillary wedge pressure (LV preload surrogate).  
- **PADP:** Pulmonary arterial diastolic pressure.  

### Vascular Resistance  
- **SVR:** Systemic vascular resistance.  
- **PVR:** Pulmonary vascular resistance—critical in RV failure.

# 3. Echocardiography Terminology

### LV Function  
- **EF:** Ejection fraction (imperfect measure of contractility).  
- **LVOT VTI:** Velocity-time integral; best surrogate for stroke volume.  
- **E/e’:** LV filling pressure estimate.  
- **A-line fusion:** Tachycardia-related diastolic abnormality.

### RV Function  
- **TAPSE:** Tricuspid annular plane systolic excursion.  
- **RV S':** Tissue Doppler S-wave velocity.  
- **FAC:** RV fractional area change.  
- **RVEDA/LVEDA:** Ratio of end-diastolic areas; >0.6 suggests RV overload.  
- **Septal D-sign:** RV pressure overload causing septal flattening.

### Other Terms  
- **IVC collapsibility/distensibility:** Preload estimation.  
- **Pericardial effusion with RA/RV collapse:** Tamponade physiology.  
- **LVOT obstruction:** Systolic anterior motion (SAM) + gradient elevation.

# 4. Ventilator Terminology

### Fundamental Variables  
- **Vt:** Tidal volume.  
- **RR:** Respiratory rate.  
- **PEEP:** Positive end-expiratory pressure.  
- **Pplat:** Plateau pressure (alveolar pressure).  
- **PIP:** Peak inspiratory pressure.  
- **ΔP (Driving Pressure):** Pplat – PEEP (key mortality predictor).  
- **Cstat (Static Compliance):** Vt / (Pplat – PEEP).  
- **MV:** Minute ventilation (RR × Vt).  

### Modes  
- **VCV/PRVC:** Volume control, pressure-regulated volume control.  
- **PCV:** Pressure control.  
- **PSV:** Pressure support ventilation.  
- **APRV:** Airway pressure release ventilation.  
- **HFNC:** High-flow nasal cannula.  
- **NIV (BiPAP):** Non-invasive ventilation.

### Vent-Mechanics Terms  
- **Auto-PEEP:** Air trapping due to inadequate exhalation time.  
- **Recruitment:** Opening of collapsed alveoli.  
- **Overdistension:** Excessive stretching; worsens ΔP and causes VILI.  
- **VILI:** Ventilator-induced lung injury.

# 5. ARDS & Respiratory Physiology Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **PF Ratio** | PaO₂/FiO₂ ratio; defines ARDS severity |
| **Shunt** | Non-oxygenated blood mixing with oxygenated blood |
| **V/Q Mismatch** | Ventilation-perfusion imbalance |
| **Dead Space** | Ventilated but non-perfused lung |
| **Proning** | Ventilation strategy: improves V/Q matching & RV load |
| **NMB** | Neuromuscular blockade used for severe ARDS or dyssynchrony |

ARDS Severity:  
- **Mild:** PF 200–300  
- **Moderate:** PF 100–200  
- **Severe:** PF < 100

# 6. Neurocritical Care Terminology

### Core Terms  
- **ICP:** Intracranial pressure.  
- **CPP:** Cerebral perfusion pressure = MAP – ICP.  
- **CBF:** Cerebral blood flow.  
- **Cerebral Autoregulation:** CBF maintained despite MAP changes (impaired in SAH/TBI).  
- **Herniation Syndrome:** Downward or lateral displacement of brain structures.  

### Monitoring  
- **EVD:** External ventricular drain for CSF drainage.  
- **PbtO₂:** Brain tissue oxygenation.  
- **TCD:** Transcranial Doppler (for vasospasm surveillance).  

### Pathology  
- **Vasospasm:** Narrowing of cerebral arteries after SAH.  
- **Takotsubo (Stress-induced CM):** Often co-occurs with neurologic injury.  
- **Decorticate/Decerebrate:** Brainstem involvement indicators.

# 7. Sedation, Analgesia & Delirium Terminology

### Sedation  
- **RASS:** Richmond Agitation Sedation Scale.  
- **PADIS:** SCCM guidelines for Pain, Agitation, Delirium, Immobility, Sleep.  
- **Light Sedation:** RASS –1 to 0.  
- **Deep Sedation:** RASS –3 to –4.  

### Drugs & Concepts  
- **Context-Sensitive Half-Time:** Duration increases with infusion time.  
- **OFA/OFA Pathway:** Opioid-free anesthesia or analgesia approach.  
- **NMB:** Neuromuscular blockade (cisatracurium common).  

### Delirium  
- **CAM-ICU:** Confusion Assessment Method.  
- **ICU Delirium:** Combination of inflammation, neurotransmitter imbalance, sleep disruption.

# 8. Cardiac Surgery & CTICU Terminology

### Common Post-Op Terms  
- **CABG:** Coronary artery bypass graft.  
- **AVR/MVR:** Aortic/mitral valve replacement.  
- **CPB:** Cardiopulmonary bypass.  
- **Cross-Clamp Time:** Aortic occlusion duration.  
- **Rewarming:** Phase post-CPB where hemodynamics often deteriorate.  

### Post-Cardiotomy Complications  
- **Vasoplegia:** Low SVR after CPB.  
- **LCOS:** Low cardiac output syndrome.  
- **Tamponade:** Pericardial compression impairing diastolic filling.  
- **RV Failure:** Common after CPB due to ischemia or elevated PVR.

# 9. ECMO Terminology

### Modes  
- **VA-ECMO:** Veno-arterial ECMO (cardiac + respiratory support).  
- **VV-ECMO:** Veno-venous ECMO (respiratory support only).  

### Key Metrics  
- **Flow:** ECMO pump output.  
- **Sweep:** CO₂ removal control.  
- **Oxygenator:** Gas exchange membrane.  
- **LV Unloading:** Necessity in VA-ECMO to prevent LV distention.

### Complications  
- **Harlequin Syndrome:** Differential hypoxia in VA-ECMO.  
- **Oxygenator Clotting:** Requires anticoagulation vigilance.

# 10. Laboratory & Physiology Abbreviations

### Blood Gases  
- **PaO₂:** Arterial oxygen tension.  
- **PaCO₂:** Arterial CO₂ tension.  
- **A–a Gradient:** Alveolar-arterial oxygen difference.  
- **BE:** Base excess.  

### Renal  
- **AKI:** Acute kidney injury.  
- **FENa / FEUrea:** Fractional excretion tests for AKI differentiation.  

### Hematology  
- **Hgb:** Hemoglobin.  
- **Plt:** Platelets.  
- **INR/PTT:** Coagulation parameters.

# 11. Rounds Communication Shorthand

| Abbreviation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| **HD stable** | Hemodynamically stable |
| **Wean FiO₂** | Reduce supplemental oxygen as tolerated |
| **Escalate PEEP** | Increase PEEP per ARDS strategy |
| **MAP ≥ 65** | Maintain MAP 65+ for perfusion |
| **Off sedation holiday** | Daily awakening trial |
| **Plan to extubate** | Team intent to liberate from ventilator once criteria met |
| **Net –1L** | Net negative fluid balance of 1 liter |
| **Check lactate trend** | Serial lactate for perfusion monitoring |

# 12. Essential One-Line Physiology Definitions (High-Yield for Rounds)

- **Driving Pressure:** Most important predictor of ARDS mortality.  
- **RV Failure:** Preload-dependent, afterload-intolerant state.  
- **CPP:** Cannot be optimized without understanding cardiac function.  
- **Proning:** Improves oxygenation *and* reduces RV strain.  
- **Vasoplegia:** Shock state characterized by pathologic loss of vascular tone.  
- **High PEEP:** Improves oxygenation but worsens RV afterload.  
- **Echo Guidance:** Superior to static pressures for fluid decisions.  
- **SCV/SvO₂:** Global balance of DO₂ and VO₂.
