## Patient 1 — POD0 CABG ×3, Vasoplegic-Predominant Shock + Hypothermia + Moderate Bleeding

**Status:**
- MAP 58–62 on NE 0.10 + vaso 0.03
- SVR 640, CI 2.1
- Temp 35.8°C
- Chest tube: 140 mL/hr
- Lactate 2.9 → 3.4 rising
- CVP 8

**Assessment:** Vasoplegia likely predominant but LV contribution not excluded; hypothermia worsening vasodilation and coagulopathy. Moderate ongoing bleeding.

**Plan:**
- Immediate bedside TTE (LV function, RV function, preload, septal position)
- If EF >40% → continue dual vasopressors; if CI <2.2 → add epi 0.02–0.03 µg/kg/min
- Rewarm to >36.5°C (forced air + warmed fluids). Expect SVR improvement
- Preload: small bolus 250 mL only if echo shows underfilling
- Hourly chest tube checks; transfuse to maintain Hgb >8, fibrinogen >150
- Delay extubation until warm, minimal pressor, lactate improving

**Overnight Goals:** MAP ≥65, CI >2.2, lactate trending down, temp >36.5°C, chest tube <100 mL/hr, transition toward single pressor.

## Patient 2 — VA-ECMO (Fulminant Viral Myocarditis), Rising Lactate + Concern for LV Distension + Early Circuit Failure

**Status:**
- ECMO flow 4.0 L/min
- Lactate 4.1 → 5.3 rising
- SvO₂ 58%
- UOP 20 mL/hr
- LDH 610 + widening pre/post oxygenator gradient

**Assessment:** Global DO₂ inadequate despite ECMO; high suspicion for LV distension, hemolysis, or membrane dysfunction.

**Plan:**
- Urgent bedside TTE to assess LV distension, AV opening, RV function
- Trial increase flows to 4.3–4.5 L/min if tolerated
- Optimize LV unloading: afterload reduction (clevidipine/nitroprusside), early consideration of IABP or Impella if persistent distension
- Circuit check: plasma-free Hgb, haptoglobin; perfusion assessment for membrane thrombus — oxygenator change-out if gradient widens
- Start CRRT for metabolic control and renal venous congestion
- Maintain MAP 65–70 on NE ± vasopressin

**Overnight Goals:** Lactate ↓10% in 4 hrs, SvO₂ ≥65%, no LV distension, stable ECMO gradients, UOP improving or CRRT running.
